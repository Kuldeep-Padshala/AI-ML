# 📚 Resources

> *"Code teaches you how to build models. Resources teach you why they work."*

Welcome to the knowledge base of this repository.

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning are built on decades of mathematical discoveries, research, experimentation, and innovation. While writing code is essential, developing a deep understanding requires studying concepts from books, research papers, documentation, and educational materials.

This directory serves as a carefully curated collection of resources that support every stage of my learning journey.

Unlike many repositories that simply collect hundreds of PDFs, the objective here is quality over quantity. Every resource included has been chosen because it contributed meaningfully to my understanding of a concept or solved a specific learning challenge.

---

## Why this folder exists

Learning AI is not about memorizing library functions.

It is about understanding:

* the mathematics behind algorithms,
* the intuition that makes them work,
* the research that introduced them,
* and the practical techniques used by engineers and researchers.

This folder helps bridge the gap between theory and implementation.

---

## What you'll find here

Rather than code, this directory contains learning materials such as:

* Mathematics references
* Roadmaps and study plans
* Books
* Research papers
* Interview preparation material
* Cheat sheets
* Helpful documentation

Each subfolder focuses on a different aspect of AI and Machine Learning education.

---

## How to use these resources

These materials are **not intended to be read sequentially**.

Instead, revisit them whenever you're learning a new topic.

For example:

* Studying Linear Regression? Review probability and statistics.
* Learning Neural Networks? Refresh calculus and optimization.
* Reading a research paper? Refer to the mathematics notes when necessary.

Over time, this collection becomes a personal reference library that grows alongside the repository.

---

## Philosophy

This folder reflects an important belief:

> **Learning AI isn't about collecting resources—it's about understanding the right ones.**

The goal isn't to own the largest collection of books or papers.

The goal is to build a collection worth returning to.
